LEGO

A Breif History

The Early Days

  • 1932: LEGO Founder, Ole Kirk Christiansen, began
  • 1934: Ole Kirk held a naming contest within the company, shortly LEGO was born
  • 1947: Following WWII, Ole Kirk was able to invest in a plastic injection molding machine.
  • 1949: LEGO starts producing plastic bricks similar to other Toy companies of the era
  • 1955: Retooling of the brand led to the LEGO System Era, with the "Town Plan" as its focal model

Innovations (1960-1969)

  • 1960: Sadly, a warehouse fire destroyed most of the wooden toy inventory, paving the way for the plastic line to take over
  • 1961: LEGO started its very interesting partnership with the Samsonite company to gain access and exposure to the North American market
  • 1962: The first LEGO wheels come rolling into production!
  • 1964: A more stable ABS plastic finally replaced the fical cellulose acetate.
  • 1966: The LEGO Train series is released, originally powered by 4.5 volts.
  • 1968: LEGOLAND Billund Opens.
  • 1969: BOOM!! Duplo.

Expansion (1970-1991)

  • 1972: LEGO introduces the first floating hulls, allowing out LEGO boats to float!!
  • 1974: Enfield, Connecticut opens the first LEGO plant in North America.
  • 1974: Human figures were added to sets, a precurser to the "Minifigure".
  • 1975: In an effort to expand the range of products, the "Expert Series" was released.
  • 1978: The "Minifigure" is released...then...
  • 1978: LEGO Space...NUFF Said!
  • 1981: The second generation of LEGO Trains are released
  • 1982: Expert Series is renamed to LEGO Technic.
